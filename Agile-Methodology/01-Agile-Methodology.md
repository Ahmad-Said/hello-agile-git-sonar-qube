## What is Agile?

Agile is an iterative approach to software development and project management
that helps teams deliver value to their customers faster and with fewer
headaches. Instead of betting everything on a single "big bang" launch, Agile
delivers work in small, consumable increments. Requirements, plans, and results
are evaluated continuously, so teams can respond to change quickly.

## The Agile Manifesto

Agile is built on four core values (2001):

1. **Individuals and interactions** over processes and tools
2. **Working software** over comprehensive documentation
3. **Customer collaboration** over contract negotiation
4. **Responding to change** over following a plan

> While there is value in the items on the right, Agile values the items on the
> left more.

## The 12 Principles (summary)

- Satisfy the customer through early and continuous delivery.
- Welcome changing requirements, even late in development.
- Deliver working software frequently (weeks rather than months).
- Business people and developers work together daily.
- Build projects around motivated individuals.
- Favor face-to-face conversation.
- Working software is the primary measure of progress.
- Promote sustainable development (a constant pace).
- Continuous attention to technical excellence.
- Simplicity — maximizing the amount of work not done.
- Self-organizing teams produce the best designs.
- Regularly reflect and adjust (retrospectives).

## Common Agile Frameworks

### Scrum
- Work organized into fixed-length **sprints** (usually 1–4 weeks).
- Roles: **Product Owner**, **Scrum Master**, **Development Team**.
- Ceremonies: Sprint Planning, Daily Stand-up, Sprint Review, Retrospective.
- Artifacts: Product Backlog, Sprint Backlog, Increment.

### Kanban
- Visualize work on a board with columns (To Do, In Progress, Done).
- Limit **Work In Progress (WIP)** to improve flow.
- Continuous delivery — no fixed-length iterations required.

### Others
- Extreme Programming (XP), Lean, SAFe (scaled Agile).

## Key Concepts

| Term | Meaning |
|------|---------|
| User Story | A short description of a feature from the user's perspective. |
| Epic | A large body of work broken into multiple stories. |
| Backlog | Prioritized list of work to be done. |
| Velocity | Amount of work a team completes in a sprint. |
| Story Points | Relative estimate of effort/complexity. |
| Definition of Done | Shared checklist for when work is complete. |

## Benefits

- Faster delivery of value.
- Greater flexibility to adapt to change.
- Improved product quality through continuous feedback.
- Higher customer satisfaction and team morale.
